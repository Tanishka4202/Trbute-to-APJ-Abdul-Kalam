A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the 11th President of India and a pioneer of the country's space and missile programmes

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India. He inspired millions with his dedication to science, education, and a developed India.

Here’s a timeline of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam’s life and achievements:


    Early Life (1931–1950)

    • 1931 (October 15): Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in a modest family.
    • 1940s: Completed schooling at Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram.

    Education (1950–1954)

    • 1950: Enrolled at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and graduated with a degree in Physics.
    • 1954: Pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai.

    Early Career in Aerospace (1954–1963)

    • 1958: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).
    • 1960s: Played a significant role in the development of a hovercraft prototype.

    ISRO and Satellite Launch Program (1963–1980)

    • 1963: Joined the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).
    • 1969: Appointed Project Director of SLV-III, India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle.
    • 1980: Successfully launched the Rohini satellite into near-Earth orbit.

    Contributions to Defense and Missiles (1980–1990)

    • 1980s: Spearheaded the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP).
    • 1989: Played a key role in the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi.

    Chief Scientific Advisor and Pokhran-II (1990–2000)

    • 1992–1999: Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO.
    • 1998: Played a pivotal role in the nuclear tests conducted at Pokhran, establishing India as a nuclear power.

    Presidency and Beyond (2002–2015)

    • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India; popularly known as the "People’s President."
    • 2007: Completed his term as President and returned to teaching and writing.
    • 2007–2015: Engaged with youth through lectures, authored books like Wings of Fire, and advocated for a developed India by 2020.

    Passing and Legacy (2015)

    • 2015 (July 27): Passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong.
    • Legacy: Revered as the "Missile Man of India" and remembered for his vision, humility, and dedication to science and education.
